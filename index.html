Hey, I'm Pavan Gaonkar! I am very much fascinated by how the universe works in the most fundamental way. As a result I really like quantum mechanics and General theory of relativity. Although I haven't studied GTR yet, I plan to dive into it soon.

Recently, I've also started developing a fascination for pure mathematics. What truly amazes me is how abstract mathematical concepts—ones that seem completely disconnected from reality, like linear algebra and differential geometry—end up having profound applications in physics. Take linear algebra, for instance. If you read Linear Algebra Done Right, you might wonder how such abstract ideas could ever be useful. But once you encounter quantum mechanics, you realize that without linear algebra, constructing quantum theory would be nearly impossible.

I recently completed my undergraduate studies at Karnataka University, Dharwad, India. I love discussing deep ideas about the universe.

Beyond physics, I enjoy reading S. L. Bhyrappa's novels, which offer profound insights into Indian philosophy and life. I also used to play ball badminton (BBT), though I no longer have the equipment or people to play with. One habit that remains unchanged, however, is my love for stargazing—I watch the stars every night because they are truly mesmerizing.

My long-term goal is to contribute original ideas to physics and possibly pursue a career in theoretical physics or academia.
