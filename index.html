About Me

Hey, I’m Pavan Gaonkar!

I developed an interest in physics despite the best efforts of my teachers. My curiosity about how the universe operates at its most fundamental level has led me to explore areas like quantum mechanics and general relativity (GTR). Although I haven’t studied GTR yet, I plan to dive into it soon.

Recently, I’ve also started developing a fascination for pure mathematics. It amazes me how abstract mathematical concepts—ones that seem disconnected from reality, like linear algebra and differential geometry—end up having profound applications in physics.

I recently completed my undergraduate studies at Karnataka University, Dharwad, India. I love discussing deep ideas about the universe—even with people who may not share the same enthusiasm.

Beyond physics, I enjoy reading S. L. Bhyrappa’s novels, playing ball badminton (though I rarely get the chance now), and stargazing every night.

My long-term goal is to contribute original ideas to physics and possibly pursue a career in theoretical physics or academia.

If you share a passion for physics, mathematics, philosophy, or the mysteries of the cosmos, I’d love to connect and exchange ideas!
